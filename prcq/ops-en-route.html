En-route ATFM delay per flight

EUROCONTROL area

Total en-route delay by reported ATFM delay cause

EUROCONTROL area
En-route ATFM delays in the EUROCONTROL area increased for the third year in a row in 2016. Total en-route ATFM delays, for the EUROCONTROL area, increased by +20.9% in 2016 which corresponds to 0.86 minutes (51 seconds) of en-route ATFM delay per flight (0.73 in 2015).

According to the ATFM delay classifications, as reported by the FMPs, Capacity/Staffing related issues remain by far the main driver of en-route ATFM delays (55.3%), followed by weather related delays (18.3%), ATC disruptions/ industrial actions (12.3%), and Event related delays (9.1%) which also include delays due to ATC system upgrades.

Share of controlled flights delayed by en-route ATFM regulations (%)


Overall, 92% of the flights in the EUROCONTROL area were not affected by ATFM delays in 2016. However, the share of flights affected by en-route ATFM delays in the EUROCONTROL area increased continuously over the past three years to reach 4.8% of the controlled flights in 2016. 3.2% of the flights were affected by airport ATFM delays in 2016.

En-route ATFM delay by ANSP

In 2016, average en-route delay per flight was highest in France (DSNA), followed by Cyprus (DCAC Cyprus), Maastricht, and Belgocontrol. Three quarters of the en-route delay were generated by four air navigation service providers: DSNA (41.6%), DFS (13.0%), Maastricht (11.4%), and ENAIRE (9%).

Horizontal en-route flight efficiency


After a continuous improvement over the past years, horizontal flight efficiency deteriorated in 2016 compared to 2015. At Pan-European level, horizontal flight efficiency in filed flight plans decreased from 95.5% in 2015 to 95.4% in 2016. At the same time, the efficiency of actual trajectories decreased stronger from 97.3% to 97.1% in 2016.
